AtomHacks is Bronx Science's annual 12-hour hackathon. Once a year, students of all skill levels come together to create something involving technology, competing for the grand prize. In previous years, students have won iPad Minis, 3D printers, Particle Maker kits, Amazon gift cards, and more.

Currently, a date has not been set for this year's hackathon. More details will be posted on the AtomHacks website as we get closer to the event. If you're curious to see what has been created in the past, see below:

  • AtomHacks III – The winner of this year's event was a visually appealing buying and selling app for college students
  • AtomHacks II – First place was a captivating music game involving the Myo gesture control armband, again.
  • AtomHacks – First place was an Android app that used the Myo to assist users with tracking fitness.
